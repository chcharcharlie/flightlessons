# Executive Request

## Product Overview
A comprehensive web-based flight instruction management system designed to help Certified Flight Instructors (CFIs) organize their teaching materials, track student progress, and ensure complete coverage of FAA Airman Certification Standards (ACS). The system provides isolated workspaces for each CFI to create customized lesson plans while maintaining standardized progress tracking across ground and flight training components. Students access their own accounts to view progress, upcoming lessons, and study materials.

## Business Objectives
- Streamline flight instruction by providing digital tools for lesson planning and progress tracking
- Ensure comprehensive ACS coverage across all training areas
- Improve student outcomes through structured progress monitoring and feedback
- Reduce administrative burden on CFIs through automated tracking and reporting
- Enhance student engagement with accessible progress visualization and study materials

## Target Users
### Primary User: Certified Flight Instructors (CFIs)
- Age: 25-65
- Tech-savvy professionals managing multiple students
- Need efficient tools for lesson planning and progress tracking
- Value customization and flexibility in teaching approach
- Require mobile access for use at airports and during flights

### Secondary User: Flight Students
- Age: 18-60
- Pursuing Private Pilot, Instrument Rating, or Commercial certificates
- Need clear visibility into their progress and upcoming requirements
- Value accessible study materials and structured learning paths
- Require mobile access for studying on-the-go

## Core Functionality
1. **Study Item Management** (Priority: High)
   - Hierarchical organization of study areas and items
   - Ground vs. flight item categorization
   - ACS mapping for each item
   - Reference material attachments

2. **Progress Tracking** (Priority: High)
   - Ground items: 3-state tracking (not taught, needs reinforcement, learned)
   - Flight items: 1-5 proficiency scale
   - Historical progress visualization
   - Per-item progress history

3. **Lesson Planning & Execution** (Priority: High)
   - Template lesson plan creation
   - Actual lesson creation from templates
   - Post-lesson scoring and notes
   - Flight route planning (basic)

4. **Student Management** (Priority: High)
   - Individual student accounts
   - CFI-student associations
   - Progress dashboards for students
   - Shared lesson plans and materials

5. **ACS Coverage Analysis** (Priority: High)
   - Map items to ACS requirements
   - Gap analysis and reporting
   - Coverage tracking for Private, Instrument, and Commercial

6. **Mobile Web Interface** (Priority: Medium)
   - Responsive design for tablets and phones
   - Optimized for field use
   - Quick access to scoring and notes

## Technical Requirements
- Platform: Web-based application with mobile-responsive design
- Integrations: None initially (future: potential flight planning APIs)
- Security: Secure authentication and data isolation per CFI
- Performance: Fast loading for mobile use in variable connectivity
- Data: Reliable storage with backup capabilities

## Constraints
- Timeline: MVP within 3-4 months
- Budget: Self-funded project, minimize external service costs
- Regulatory: Must align with current FAA ACS standards
- Technical: Must work reliably on mobile devices with limited connectivity

## Success Metrics
- CFI Adoption: 10+ active CFIs within 6 months
- Student Engagement: 80% of students accessing weekly
- Progress Tracking: 100% of lessons documented with scores
- ACS Coverage: Complete mapping for supported certificates
- User Satisfaction: 4.5+ rating from users
